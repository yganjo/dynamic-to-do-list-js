tomato farming

Tomato farming involves cultivating tomato plants for their edible fruits, which are widely used in cooking. It requires well-drained, fertile soil and consistent sunlight. Tomatoes can be grown in open fields, greenhouses, or pots, depending on the scale and climate. Key practices include regular watering, pest and disease control, and staking or trellising to support the plants. The farming cycle generally lasts 3-4 months from planting to harvest. Tomatoes are a high-demand crop, and successful farming can yield good profits for farmers. 1
